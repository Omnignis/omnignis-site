Services

What we do.

Cybersecurity for small practices and small businesses, handled end to end — assessment, hardening, training, and ongoing protection. Plus custom software, built on request, and a specialized reporting tool for churches.

01 · Assess & Harden

Find out how you'd actually lose money — then close those doors

Security work grounded in how small businesses actually get broken into, not a checklist. You get a clear picture of your real risks and a prioritized, plain-English plan — then we do the fixes.

  • Cybersecurity risk assessment. A fixed-price review of your accounts, devices, email, and network. Start with the Cybersecurity Snapshot below — or the HIPAA Security Risk Analysis for healthcare practices.
  • Microsoft 365 / Google Workspace hardening. MFA enforced properly, legacy sign-ins closed, mail rules audited, forwarding locked down — where most real attacks start and end.
  • Email authentication & anti-fraud. SPF, DKIM, and DMARC done all the way, so criminals can't send invoices as you — plus the payment-change process rule that stops wire fraud cold.
  • Backup, network segmentation & remote-access lockdown. Backup that provably restores, guest wifi separated from business systems, and remote access closed to the internet.
  • Cyber insurance readiness. Your carrier's questionnaire answered accurately, gaps fixed before they become a denied claim.
  • Staff security training. Short, practical sessions on phishing and payment fraud — meets Texas HB 300 training requirements for healthcare workplaces.
02 · Ongoing Protection

Keep it secure, month after month

Security isn't done when the fixes ship. A simple monthly program keeps your posture current — and gives you a named technical person in your corner whenever a decision comes up.

  • Annual security program. Yearly assessment refresh, quarterly account and access reviews, training records kept, and your insurance renewal handled.
  • Managed detection & reporting. Alerting deployed and tuned, reviewed weekly, reported monthly — business hours, next-business-day response, in writing.
  • Security advisor (vCISO) retainer. Monthly check-ins, policy upkeep, vendor and quote reviews, and the answer when a client, carrier, or regulator asks "who handles your cybersecurity?"
  • Website care plans. Updates, backups, and uptime monitoring for the site we built you — or the one you already have.
03 · Custom Software — On Request

When the right tool doesn't exist, we build it

Omnignis is a cybersecurity practice first. But when a client needs internal tooling — a portal, an automation, a report generator — we build it ourselves, secure by default, rather than referring it out.

  • Small business websites. Designed, built, and launched: fast, secure, and easy to keep updated.
  • Custom web apps & client portals. Logins, dashboards, and tools shaped around how your business runs.
  • Workflow automation & reporting. The work you do by hand every week, made automatic — your data pulled, formatted, and emailed on a schedule.

What we don't do

A cybersecurity company you can trust should be clear about where its job ends. These three things are outside our scope — on purpose.

Not offered

Cameras, alarms & door access

Physical security systems are licensed work in Texas. We don't sell, install, service, monitor, or respond to them — and we'll refer you to a licensed integrator who does.

Not offered

24/7 emergency response

We won't promise coverage we can't deliver. Our contracts say business hours and next-business-day — and we keep a vetted incident-response firm on file for true emergencies.

Not offered

Investigations of people

Finding out who did something is licensed private-investigation work in Texas. We handle the technical side and refer attribution questions to a licensed investigator.
