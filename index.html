How small businesses actually lose money

The pattern, start to finish

Someone on your team gets a convincing email and types their password into a fake login page. The attacker signs into that mailbox and sits there quietly — reading — for three weeks. They learn who pays whom, what invoices look like, who approves what.

Then they email your bookkeeper a real-looking invoice with new banking details. The money leaves. Nobody notices for a month.

A firewall doesn't stop that. Neither does a VPN or an antivirus box. Identity controls, mail controls, and one boring process rule do. That's what we fix.

Cybersecurity risk assessment

A full review of how your business could lose money or data, delivered as a plain-English report with a prioritized fix list. For healthcare practices, this is the HIPAA Security Risk Analysis federal law already requires.

Microsoft 365 / Google Workspace hardening

MFA enforced properly, legacy sign-in methods closed, mail rules audited, forwarding locked down. This is where most real-world attacks on small business start — and end.

Cyber insurance readiness

Your carrier's questionnaire, answered accurately. We check what you actually have against what you're attesting to, and fix the gaps before they become a denied claim.

Staff security training

Short, practical sessions your team will actually remember — phishing, payment fraud, and the handful of habits that stop most attacks. Meets Texas HB 300 training requirements for healthcare workplaces.

Backup that actually restores

Microsoft and Google don't back up your data — they keep it available until something deletes it. We set up real backup, then prove it restores.

Network segmentation & firewall

What we don't do

A cybersecurity company you can trust should be clear about where its job ends. These three things are outside our scope — on purpose.

Not offered

Cameras, alarms & door access

Physical security systems are licensed work in Texas. We don't sell, install, service, monitor, or respond to them — and we'll refer you to a licensed integrator who does.

Not offered

24/7 emergency response

We're a small practice and we won't promise coverage we can't deliver. Our contracts say business hours and next-business-day — and we keep a vetted incident-response firm on file for true emergencies.

Not offered

Investigations of people

Finding out who did something is licensed private-investigation work in Texas. We handle the technical side and refer attribution questions to a licensed investigator.
